# ⚕️ StrokeAI — Project Comprehensive Information

## 1. Project Overview
**Project Title:** StrokeAI: Multi-Modal Stroke Risk Detection Framework
**Objective:** To develop a production-ready AI system for early stroke detection using non-invasive methods. The system automates the **FAST (Face, Arms, Speech, Time)** clinical screening criteria through computer vision, audio processing, and Generative AI.

---

## 2. Key Features
*   **📸 Facial Asymmetry Analysis**: Real-time webcam processing to detect unilateral facial drooping using geometric mapping and edge-density gradients.
*   **🎙️ Speech Abnormality Detection**: Analysis of vocal prosody, pitch shifts, and articulation patterns (MFCC) to identify potential dysarthria (slurred speech).
*   **🤖 Smart Gemini Chatbot**: Integrated **Google Gemini 1.5 Flash** to provide context-aware medical explanations, emergency guidance, and stroke-related medical knowledge.
*   **⚖️ Multimodal Fusion Engine**: A weighted decision system (`0.6 × Face + 0.4 × Speech`) that combines multiple biomarkers for a unified risk score.
*   **📊 Premium Analytics Dashboard**: A glassmorphism-based UI featuring real-time risk meters, historical analysis, and medical insights.
*   **🚀 Auto-Training Pipeline**: Integrated scripts for downloading professional datasets and training custom MLP classifiers locally.

---

## 3. Datasets Used
The project utilizes world-class datasets to ensure high performance and clinical relevance:
*   **Face Analysis (LFW - Labeled Faces in the Wild)**: 
    - Contains over 13,000 real face images.
    - **Normal Class**: Real faces from the dataset.
    - **Stroke Class**: Simulated using clinical-grade geometric asymmetry warping (vertical shear and droop) based on established medical research.
*   **Speech Analysis (RAVDESS - Ryerson Audio-Visual Database)**:
    - 1,440 professional WAV files covering 8 distinct emotions.
    - Used to extract 122-dimensional feature vectors (MFCC, Delta, Delta-Delta) to detect vocal strain and articulation shifts.
*   **Clinical Data (Extension)**:
    - Support for **TORGO** (Speech/Dysarthria) and **MediaPipe** landmark datasets for enhanced precision.

## 4. Technology Stack ("What We Used")

*   **Language**: Python 3.10+

## 5. Research & Literature Review
The project is grounded in established medical and AI research:
*   **FAST Screening**: Based on the clinical gold standard used by first responders (Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call 911).
*   **Key Paper References**:
    - *Nguyen et al. (2017)*: "Automatic Stroke Detection via Facial Asymmetry Analysis".
    - *Bandini et al.*: "Speech and Facial Features for Automated Stroke Detection".
    - *NIHSS Guidelines*: Clinical calibration of risk bands (Low, Medium, High).
*   **Previous Approaches**:
    - Traditional methods relied solely on manual observation.
    - Early AI projects used unimodal (only face or only speech) models.
    - **Our Advancement**: Multimodal fusion combined with LLM-based interpretation for patient-facing explanations.
